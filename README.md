# 📊 E-Commerce Customer Behavior & RFM Segmentation
## Unlocking Growth Through Data-Driven Loyalty and Retention Strategies

---

## 📌 Project Background

An online retail leader in the UK faced challenges in optimizing marketing spend and identifying high-value growth opportunities. Without a clear understanding of **customer lifetime value** or purchasing lifecycles, the business struggled to distinguish between **one-time shoppers** and **long-term brand advocates**.

### 🎯 The Mission

Transform raw transactional data into actionable intelligence by:

- Segmenting the customer base using RFM analysis
- Identifying revenue concentration patterns
- Detecting high-value customer segments
- Building a framework for personalized retention strategies

## 🧱 Data Structure & ETL

The dataset consists of transactional retail records from a UK-based e-commerce company, including:

- Invoice numbers
- Product stock codes
- Customer identifiers
- Transaction timestamps
- Quantity and unit price

### Schema Logic

The analysis follows a **customer-centric analytical structure**:

1. Raw transactional dataset cleaned and standardized
2. Invalid stock codes and non-product transactions removed
3. Duplicate invoices eliminated
4. Negative quantities filtered (returns excluded)
5. Monetary values calculated
6. Aggregation into a Customer-Level RFM table

Final analytical dataset includes:

| Metric | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Recency | Days since last purchase |
| Frequency | Number of total orders |
| Monetary | Total customer spend |

## 📈 Executive Summary & Visual Insights

### 1️⃣ Strategic Shift Toward Customer Retention

📊 *Returning customers accounted for over **80% of total activity in November**, while new customer acquisition stabilized at **19.4%**.*

This indicates strong loyalty formation and improving revenue stability through repeat purchasing behavior.

---

### 2️⃣ Geographical Concentration

📊 *The United Kingdom dominates overall market share and purchasing activity.*

International expansion represents a **long-term growth opportunity**, while the primary short-term strategy should focus on optimizing the UK customer lifecycle.

---

### 3️⃣ RFM Customer Segmentation

📊 *"Champions" generate the highest revenue contribution despite not being the largest segment.*

Meanwhile:

**Potential Loyalists** represent the strongest conversion opportunity into high-value repeat customers.

---

### 4️⃣ Revenue Concentration

📊 *Approximately **74% of total revenue** is generated by the top **20% of customers**.*

This confirms strong dependence on power users and highlights the importance of protecting high-value segments through targeted retention strategies.

---

## 🚀 Strategic Recommendations

### 🏆 VIP Retention Strategy

Introduce an exclusive **Elite Loyalty Tier** for the **Champions segment**, including:

- Early product access
- Priority support
- Personalized promotions

This protects the **74% revenue core**.

---

### 📧 Potential Loyalist Conversion

Deploy personalized email triggers for customers with:

- High recency
- Moderate purchase frequency

Encourage progression toward their **3rd and 4th purchases**.

---

### 🎯 Frequency Incentive Program

Since most customers place **1–5 orders**, introduce:

- Points-based reward system
- Purchase streak bonuses
- Repeat-purchase discounts

Goal: Increase average order frequency.

---

### 🔄 At-Risk Customer Re-engagement

Launch targeted campaigns such as:

**"We Miss You" discount offers**

to reduce churn risk before migration into the **Lost segment**.
